The Message

One Lord. One Faith. One Baptism.

The unchanging gospel preached by the Apostles — that Jesus Christ is the fullness of God, that there is no other Name by which we must be saved, and that His Spirit indwells those who obey.

I · The Identity

Jesus is God

The fullness of the Godhead in bodily form.

The eternal Father revealed in the Son. The Word made flesh. The Almighty One who came in the likeness of man to redeem us — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are the one and only God revealed through Jesus Christ.

Colossians 2:9 · 1 Timothy 3:16
II · The Salvation

Salvation in His Name

No other Name. No other way.

Salvation is not earned by works, religion, or human merit. It is found exclusively in the crucified, buried, and risen Jesus Christ — His blood, His Name, His finished work on the cross.

Acts 4:12 · John 14:6
III · The Spirit

The Holy Spirit

A real baptism, a real fire, a real life.

The same Spirit that filled the apostles in Acts 2 still fills hungry hearts today. He is not a metaphor — He is the very presence of God indwelling, empowering, sanctifying, and sealing the believer.

Acts 2:4 · Romans 8:9
Acts 2:38 · The Plan

How to be saved.

When the multitude on the Day of Pentecost asked the apostles 'What shall we do?', Peter gave a clear, three-fold answer. It has not changed.

01
Acts 2:38a

Repent

Turn from sin. Turn toward God.

Repentance is more than feeling sorry — it is a decisive turning. Acknowledge that you have sinned, choose to abandon that life, and ask Jesus Christ for forgiveness. He is faithful and just to forgive every soul that comes to Him in honesty.

'Repent ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out.' — Acts 3:19
02
Acts 2:38b

Be baptized in the Name of Jesus

A burial with Him. A new birth in Him.

Water baptism by full immersion, invoking the saving Name of Jesus Christ, is the apostolic pattern from Pentecost forward. It is not a symbol added later — it is the moment your old life is buried and Christ Himself washes away your sins.

'And now why tarriest thou? arise, and be baptized, and wash away thy sins, calling on the name of the Lord.' — Acts 22:16
03
Acts 2:38c

Receive the Holy Spirit

The promise is for you, and your children, and all who are far off.

The same Spirit poured out in Acts 2 — accompanied by speaking with new tongues as the Spirit gives utterance — is still being poured out today on hungry, surrendered hearts. This is the new birth Jesus spoke of in John 3. This is the seal of the believer.

'For the promise is unto you, and to your children, and to all that are afar off.' — Acts 2:39

Who We Are

A people of the Name.

We are a conservative Apostolic congregation walking in the original gospel — preaching Jesus Christ, baptizing in His Name, and contending earnestly for the faith once delivered to the saints.

IANJ is more than a service on Sunday. It is a covenant family where the Word is honored, the Spirit is welcomed, families are strengthened, and souls meet a real and saving Jesus.
